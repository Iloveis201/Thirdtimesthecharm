Manu Ginóbili Complete Biography

Emanuel David Ginóbili Maccari, better known as Manu Ginbili, is a former Argentine professional basketball player. Further, the player is one of the top players that the league has ever seen and is remembered for his legacy. Throughout his 23-year professional career, he became one of only two players (along with Bill Bradley) to win a EuroLeague championship, an NBA championship, and an Olympic gold medal. Manu Ginobili was born on 28 July, 1977 in Bahía Blanca, Argentina. His father’s name is Jorge Ginobili, and his mother’s name is Raquel Ginobili. Manu comes from a basketball-playing family. For instance, his older brother, Leandro Ginoboli, retired in 2003 after seven years in the Argentine basketball league. Likewise, his younger brother, Sebastián Ginobili, played in the Argentine local league and the Spanish Liga Espaola de Baloncesto. Manu learned to play the game while his father, Jorge Ginobili, was a coach at a club in Baha Blanca, Argentina. The player’s passion for basketball overgrew due to the proliferation of basketball clubs in Bahia Blanca and his idolization of Michael Jordan.
